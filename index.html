Present-day of Zoroastrianism!

Muslim Conquest!
The Muslim conquest of Persia between 633 and 651 A.D. led to the fall of the Sassanian Persian Empire and the decline of the Zoroastrian religion in Iran. The Arab invaders charged Zoroastrians living in the Persia extra taxes for retaining their religious practices and implemented laws to make life difficult for them. Over time, most Iranian Zoroastrians converted to Islam.

Parsi Religion

Parsi are followers of Zoroastrianism in India. According to Parsi tradition, a group of Iranian Zoroastrians emigrated from Persia to escape religious persecution by the Muslim majority after the Arab conquest. The Parsi are an ethnic minority in India and Pakistan. Today there are about 60,000 Parsi in India and 1,400 in Pakistan.
